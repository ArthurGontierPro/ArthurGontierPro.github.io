About me

Hi there! I am Arthur Gontier. Since September 2020, I am a PhD student in symmetric cryptanalysis with declarative languages in the CAPSULE team in Rennes supervised by Stéphanie Delaune, Patrick Derbez and Charles Prud'homme. I am working on symmetric cryptanalysis problems like Cube attacks or Differential attacks. To do so, I use several solvers or even dedicated algorithms.

In this past two years, we published two papers in cryptography related conferences where we used many optimisation methods, (CP MILP SAT and dedicated algorithms). To tackle the hard problems of cryptography with CP, we often need to make new constraints. Indeed, there are very specific cryptographic properties we modeled in graph constraints or set constraints for solvers like Choco and Chuffed.

I also worked on explanations and conflict analysis. In a CP 2020 workshop, we proposed an explanation generator to help the use of explanations in CP solvers.

Finally, I participate to TAGADA, a generic tool to perform differential cryptanalysis on generic block ciphers with constraint programming. The idea is the following: anyone can model any cipher and the tool will perform differential cryptanalysis on it.

Before that, I had a Master's Degree in Nantes and Bruxelles focusing on optimisation techniques with CP teachers like Nicolas Beldiceanu and Eric Monfroy. I also did several research internships, on multi objective optimisation, static program analysis and CP explanations. Feel free to check my CV for more details.
